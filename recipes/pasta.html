Pasta Recipe

Description:

Pasta is one of the most recognizable and beloved dishes in the world, with its roots firmly planted in Italian culinary tradition.
There are hundreds of different pasta shapes, from spaghetti and penne to fettuccine and rigatoni, each designed to perfectly hold a specific type of sauce.
Regardless of the shape, the key to great pasta lies in quality ingredients and simple, honest preparation.

Making perfect pasta is not complicated, but it does require attention to detail.
The cooking water must be generously salted and the pasta cooked al dente, meaning it remains slightly firm when bitten.
Italian cooks always reserve a cup of pasta cooking water before draining, as the starch in the water helps bind the sauce to the pasta and creates a smoother, creamier texture that coats every strand perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti or pasta of your choice
  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 800g crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 100g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, then cook pasta according to package instructions until al dente, reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat, add chopped onion and cook for 3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks. Drain excess fat.
  5. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes to deepen the flavor.
  6. Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, sugar, salt and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  7. Reduce heat to low and simmer the sauce for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally until thickened.
  8. Add a splash of reserved pasta water to the sauce and stir to create a smoother, richer consistency.
  9. Add drained pasta directly into the sauce and toss well until every strand is fully coated.
  10. Serve immediately, topped with freshly grated Parmesan and fresh basil leaves.
