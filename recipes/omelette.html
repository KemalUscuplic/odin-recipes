Omelette Recipe

Description:

The omelette is one of the most versatile and quick dishes in cooking, requiring only a handful of ingredients and just a few minutes to prepare.
Originally a staple of French cuisine, the omelette has become a universal breakfast and brunch favourite enjoyed across the world.
Whether filled with cheese, vegetables, or meat, a well-made omelette is light, fluffy, and incredibly satisfying.

The secret to a perfect omelette lies in the technique and the heat.
The pan must be hot enough to cook the eggs quickly, but not so hot that they become tough and rubbery.
The eggs should be whisked vigorously to incorporate air, which gives the omelette its signature light and tender texture.
With the right balance of heat, timing, and quality fillings, even a simple omelette can be an impressive and delicious meal.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 50g cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small tomato, diced
  • 50g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 strips bacon, chopped
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps:

  1. Crack eggs into a bowl, add milk, salt and pepper, then whisk until fully combined and slightly frothy.
  2. Heat butter in a non-stick pan over medium heat until melted and lightly foaming.
  3. Add bacon and cook for 2 minutes until slightly crispy, then remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, sauté onion and mushrooms for 3 minutes until softened.
  5. Add diced tomato and cook for another minute, then remove all vegetables and set aside.
  6. Pour the egg mixture into the pan and let it sit undisturbed for 30 seconds until the edges begin to set.
  7. Gently push the cooked edges toward the center with a spatula, tilting the pan to let uncooked egg flow to the edges.
  8. When the egg is mostly set but still slightly glossy on top, add cheese, bacon and vegetables to one half.
  9. Fold the omelette in half over the filling and let it cook for another 30 seconds.
  10. Slide onto a plate, garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.
