Lasagna Recipe

Description:

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of flat pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese.
It is one of the oldest and most beloved pasta dishes in the world, originating from the Emilia-Romagna region of Italy.
The combination of slow-cooked ragù, silky béchamel, and generous amounts of Parmesan creates a deeply satisfying and hearty meal.

Making lasagna requires patience, but the result is well worth the effort.
The meat sauce is typically simmered for at least an hour to develop a deep, complex flavor, while the béchamel must be stirred constantly to achieve a smooth, creamy texture.
Once assembled and baked until golden and bubbling, lasagna is best served after resting for a few minutes, allowing the layers to set and making it easier to slice and serve.

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 800g crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 250ml béchamel sauce
  • 300g mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 100g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F) and cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat, add chopped onion and minced garlic, cook until softened.
  3. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks. Drain excess fat.
  4. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt and pepper. Simmer on low heat for 30 minutes.
  5. Prepare béchamel sauce by melting butter, whisking in flour, then slowly adding milk and stirring until smooth and thick.
  6. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a greased baking dish.
  7. Layer noodles over the sauce, then spread béchamel on top, followed by meat sauce and a generous sprinkle of both cheeses.
  8. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with béchamel and a final layer of Parmesan on top.
  9. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then remove foil and bake for another 15 minutes until golden and bubbling.
  10. Remove from oven and let rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
